Properties of Numbers

Numbers can have different characteristics which describe a number's behavior.

Even vs. Odd

One of the most basic characteristics is whether a number is even versus odd. An Even\textcolor{cyan}{\textit{Even}}Even number is divisible by 2 while an Odd\textcolor{cyan}{\textit{Odd}}Odd number is not. Even numbers are: 2,4,6,...2, 4, 6, ...2,4,6,... while odd numbers are: 1,3,5,...1, 3, 5, ...1,3,5,....

More formally, it is any number nnn such that there exists another number kkk such that: n=2kn = 2kn=2k. In other words, nnn is divisible by 222.

Likewise, odd numbers are those such that n=2k+1n = 2k+1n=2k+1. In other words, noddn_{odd}nodd​ is guaranteed to not be divisible by 222.

Prime vs. Composite

Another characteristic of a number is whether it is divisible by numbers other than itself and 1. If it may be divided by other numbers, that number is considered Composite\textcolor{cyan}{\textit{Composite}}Composite. If not, that number is Prime\textcolor{cyan}{\textit{Prime}}Prime.

In other words, a prime number is a number divisible only by itself and 1. A composite is divisible by itself, 1, and other numbers.

Every number greater than 1 is either prime or composite.

It is said that 111 is neither prime nor composite.

Prime numbers are special numbers and are the subject of a lot of studies and discoveries in mathematics, due to their complicated nature. Their discovery is not exactly straightforward, and no distinguishable pattern for each and every prime number has been found (yet?).

Types of Numbers

Numbers are described in sets, where all numbers in that set share some unique property or characteristic. Such sets are described via a special symbol which is commonly a bolded, captilalized letter often with double-lined parts of the letter, like Z\mathbb{Z}Z or Q\mathbb{Q}Q. These are the symbols used to refer to the set, and will be provided below.
